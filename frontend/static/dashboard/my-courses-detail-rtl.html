Elementary School:

Grade 1 - Math
  • Overview
  • Course Content

In Grade One, math typically focuses on building a strong foundation of basic math concepts, such as numbers, addition, subtraction, and place value. Here's an overview of the key topics that Grade One students usually cover in math:

Numbers and counting: Students learn to count to 100, recognize and write numbers up to 100, and understand the concepts of more than/less than, equal to, and odd/even.

Addition and subtraction: Students begin to learn basic addition and subtraction facts, usually up to 10 or 20. They also learn the concepts of addition and subtraction, such as joining and separating sets of objects.

Place value: Students learn to recognize and understand the value of digits in two-digit numbers, such as tens and ones.

Measurement: Students learn to measure length, weight, and capacity using non-standard units, such as using blocks to measure length.

Geometry: Students learn to identify and describe basic two-dimensional shapes, such as squares, circles, and triangles, and three-dimensional shapes, such as cubes and spheres.

Patterns and relationships: Students learn to recognize and create patterns using shapes, colors, and numbers.

Overall, the focus of Grade One math is to help students build a strong foundation of basic math skills and concepts that they can build upon in later grades.
